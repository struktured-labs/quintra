# Quintra v0.18.67 — Pocket Grid

SELECT now opens a compact, self-explaining dungeon map. The active 20–30-room
footprint appears as a faint 6×5 lattice on the left side of the Game Boy
screen. Each room is one square tile and every visible corridor comes from the
same reciprocal graph as the physical doors. Visited rooms and traversed links
brighten as the run progresses.

A permanent right-side key labels `YOU`, `ROOM`, `SIGIL`, `TRIAL`, `BOSS`, and
`RIFT` beside their cartridge-rendered icons. Cyan marks the current room,
violet marks Sigil and nonlinear Rift knowledge, and amber marks required
trials and the warned Colossus threshold. Room identities remain hidden until
visited or deliberately revealed; the complete faint footprint communicates
scale without solving the objectives.

The whole-game audit was refreshed against this ROM. A new Normal curriculum
sample confirms that difficulty still needs attended human tuning rather than
a blanket multiplier: one generic policy clears 10 of 45 live-boss fixtures,
while ordinary-room deaths cluster in a late seven-body pack and stage-three
pressure varies sharply by champion. Existing full-heal sanctuaries continue
to separate dungeon attrition from Colossus balance.

The larger 20–30-room topology still feels compact in attended play. That
feedback is now an explicit design finding: stage one is only 12 direct room
visits from entrance to boss, every room remains one 160×144 field, and adding
more counters would be padding rather than scale. Larger continuous outdoor
spaces, landmarked side wings, and true camera-travelling arenas remain the
next structural work.

The requested Penta Dragon comparison remains explicit: Quintra's nine bosses
already use 112×64 to 128×80 BG bodies with mobile OBJ weak points, but their
bounded camera drift is not yet a genuinely scrolling multi-screen arena. The
next boss-scale work should add real camera travel rather than merely enlarge
art or HP.
