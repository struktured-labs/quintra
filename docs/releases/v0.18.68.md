# Quintra v0.18.68 — Beyond the Frame

Quintra's first boss arena is now physically wider than the Game Boy screen.
The Crystal Colossus fight occupies a 224×136 world behind the 160×136
playfield viewport, with a real 0–64px horizontal camera. The champion,
enemies, bullets, melee arcs, effects, pickups, collision, and automated
controller all share the same world coordinates.

The old right wall at x=160 is now a traversable seam into a second crystal
landmark and warp well. Crystal telegraphs before jumping among three wells at
x=24, x=96, and x=176; the final one is genuinely beyond the former room. The
combat wall moved to BG column 27, and only the ordinary boss-clear unseal
turns its center into the far exit to Riftwild. Other rooms and all later boss
arenas explicitly reset to the existing 160px contract.

This is the first reusable answer to the playtest finding that 20–30-room
stages can still feel compact. It establishes world-space collision,
camera-relative OBJ projection, extended projectile travel, far-side rewards,
and nonlinear boss movement without adding fake room-count padding. Larger
continuous outdoor fields and landmarked dungeon wings can build on this path.

Live-ROM coverage crosses the old seam to x=199, reaches SCX 64, forces the
96→176 Crystal warp, holds the far wall during combat, opens the true far
door after the clear, descends to Riftwild, and verifies that the next
Colossus returns to 160px and SCX 0. Dense performance remains above its gate
at 147/180 updates with all 12 stress entities active. All 370 hash-bound
PyBoy checkpoints were regenerated, and the input-only mGBA pilot collects
observed post-boss relics and exits unsealed encounters on the changed ROM.
Duplicate passive IDs now share one inventory-presence slot while their stat
effects continue to stack, so a long run cannot exhaust the 16-slot registry
and silently lose a later boss relic's persistent mechanic.
